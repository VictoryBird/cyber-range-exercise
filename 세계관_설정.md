# Meridia — Cyber Exercise Worldbuilding

> This document defines the fictional geopolitical worldbuilding for the cybersecurity training exercise.
> All nations, organizations, names, and events are entirely fictional and bear no relation to real entities.

---

## 1. The Continent of Meridia

Meridia is a large continent comparable in size to Europe, situated in a temperate zone. The western coast is maritime and trade-oriented; the eastern interior is continental, resource-rich, and militarized. A critical chokepoint — the **Siros Strait** — separates the western maritime nations from the eastern continental bloc, making it a constant flashpoint for geopolitical tension.

### 1.1 Geopolitical Blocs

The continent is divided into two major geopolitical blocs with a long history of ideological opposition:

**Western Democratic Alliance (WDA)**
- Led by Arventa and Valdoria
- Values: Democratic governance, free trade, rule of law, technological innovation
- Military cooperation through the **Meridia Defense Pact (MDP)**

**Eastern Sovereignty Compact (ESC)**
- Led by Krasnovia, with Tarvek as a satellite state
- Values: State sovereignty, centralized authority, collective security, anti-Western ideology
- Operates **Unified Cyber Command** coordinating offensive cyber operations

## 2. Nations of Meridia

### 2.1 Republic of Valdoria (Exercise Target — Defender)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Full Name | Republic of Valdoria |
| Government | Federal democratic republic (presidential system) |
| Capital | Elaris |
| Population | ~25 million |
| GDP | $420 billion |
| Currency | Valdorian Crown (VCR) |
| Official Language | English |
| Head of State | President Elena Harwell |

**Economy:** Valdoria's economy is built on three pillars: semiconductor manufacturing, cybersecurity services, and maritime logistics. The western coastal region known as the **Silicon Coast** hosts the continent's densest concentration of tech companies and defense contractors. Valdoria exports advanced chips, encryption products, and cybersecurity consulting services to allies worldwide.

**Military:** The Valdorian Armed Forces number approximately 120,000 active personnel. Due to the nation's small size, Valdoria has invested heavily in asymmetric capabilities — particularly cyber defense and intelligence. The **Cyber Defense Command (CDC)** is a joint military-civilian organization responsible for protecting critical national infrastructure.

**Government Structure:**
- **Ministry of Interior and Safety (MOIS)** — Public administration, citizen services, complaint processing, e-government
- **Ministry of National Defense (MND)** — Armed forces, military intelligence, C4I systems, defense infrastructure
- **National Intelligence Service (NIS)** — Foreign intelligence, counterintelligence, threat attribution

**Weaknesses:**
- Small population limits human resources for IT and cybersecurity staffing
- Government IT modernization is uneven — legacy systems coexist with modern infrastructure
- Rapid tech sector growth has outpaced security governance frameworks
- Reliance on a single centralized DB architecture for government services creates a high-value target

**Key Infrastructure:**
- MOIS public portal (citizen services, complaints, notices)
- MOIS internal network (business portal, webmail, AI assistant, centralized DB)
- MND military network (defense portal, VPN gateway, document exchange, C4I systems)
- Industrial control systems (SCADA, Historian, PLC) for critical energy infrastructure

| Attribute | Detail |

| Government | Authoritarian federal republic (long-term presidential rule) |
| Capital | Volgrad |
| Population | ~80 million |

| Currency | Krasnovian Ruble (KRB) |
| Official Language | Krasnovian (English widely used in government) |
| Head of State | President Dmitri Volkov (in power since 2008) |

**Economy:** Krasnovia's economy is heavily dependent on energy exports (oil and natural gas). International sanctions imposed after the 2003 Siros Strait Crisis and subsequent aggressive actions have constrained economic growth, pushing the regime toward asymmetric strategies — particularly cyber operations — as a cost-effective means of projecting power.

**Military:** The Krasnovian Armed Forces number approximately 650,000 active personnel. Conventional military strength is significant but aging. The regime has prioritized investment in cyber warfare capabilities as a force multiplier.

**Cyber Capabilities:**
- **State Cyber Command (SCC)** — Centralized offensive cyber operations directorate
- **Krasnovia Security Bureau (KSB)** — Intelligence agency (comparable to a combined CIA/NSA). Houses multiple APT units
- **KSB Unit 2600** — The elite cyber operations unit responsible for **IRON VEIL** campaigns. Specializes in long-term persistent access, critical infrastructure targeting, and intelligence collection against Valdoria and its allies

**Strategic Objectives:**
1. Undermine Valdoria's technological and economic advantages
2. Collect military intelligence on Valdoria's defense posture
3. Demonstrate capability to disrupt Valdoria's critical infrastructure as a deterrent
4. Support Tarvek's destabilization operations against Western-aligned nations

**Background:** Tarvek underwent a revolution in 1979 that overthrew its pro-Western monarchy and installed a Krasnovia-aligned revolutionary government. Since then, Tarvek has served as Krasnovia's most reliable regional ally and proxy.

**Economy:** Crippled by decades of international sanctions led by Valdoria and Arventa, Tarvek's legitimate economy is severely constrained. The regime has tacitly encouraged cybercrime operations within its borders as a source of foreign currency. Cybercriminal organizations operate with impunity in Tarvek, provided they do not target domestic systems and share a portion of proceeds with the regime.

**Cyber Role:**
- **GORGON** — A Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) operation based in Tarvek. The Tarvek government provides legal safe harbor in exchange for occasional operational cooperation
- GORGON serves as a force multiplier for Krasnovia's strategic objectives — deploying ransomware as a smokescreen during IRON VEIL intelligence operations
- Tarvek's intelligence service provides GORGON with targeting intelligence from KSB

## 4. Threat Actor Profiles

### 4.1 IRON VEIL — State-Sponsored APT

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Classification | Nation-State APT |
| Sponsor | Krasnovia Security Bureau (KSB) — Unit 2600 |
| Active Since | 2017 (first detected 2022) |
| Primary Targets | Valdoria government, military, defense contractors, critical infrastructure |
| Motivation | Intelligence collection, military advantage, infrastructure disruption |

**Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs):**
- **Initial Access:** Web application exploitation (API vulnerabilities, file upload bypass), supply chain compromise
- **Persistence:** Custom implants (Go-based), scheduled tasks, SSH key deployment
- **Lateral Movement:** Credential harvesting (Mimikatz, DCSync), AD exploitation, internal phishing
- **Collection:** Database exfiltration (COPY TO), document theft, AI prompt injection for intelligence extraction
- **Impact:** C4I data manipulation, SCADA sensor data falsification, operational deception

**Tools:**
- **Sliver** — Primary C2 framework for Linux server operations
- **Havoc** — Secondary C2 for Windows endpoint operations (Demon agent)
- Custom Python scripts for OT/SCADA interaction

**Operational Pattern:** IRON VEIL operates in deliberate, multi-phase campaigns over weeks to months. They prioritize stealth and long-term access over immediate impact. The group coordinates with GORGON to create diversionary chaos during critical phases.

### 4.2 GORGON — Ransomware-as-a-Service

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Classification | Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) |
| Base of Operations | Tarvek Republic (government-tolerated) |
| Active Since | 2023 |
| Primary Targets | Healthcare, government, critical infrastructure (no targeting restrictions except Tarvek/Krasnovia) |
| Motivation | Financial (ransom + data extortion) + Strategic (Krasnovia-aligned objectives) |

**Operational Model:**
- GORGON operates as a RaaS platform: core developers maintain the ransomware and leak site, while affiliates (including state-aligned operators) conduct intrusions
- Revenue split: 70% to affiliate, 30% to GORGON platform
- Double extortion: File encryption + threatened data publication on "GORGON Leaks" dark web portal

**Relationship with IRON VEIL:**
- IRON VEIL provides initial access intelligence (compromised credentials, network maps) to GORGON affiliates
- GORGON deploys ransomware to Valdoria's public sector simultaneously with IRON VEIL's military operations
- The ransomware chaos diverts Valdoria's incident response resources away from the military network, where IRON VEIL operates undetected

## 5. Exercise Scenario Background

### 5.1 Situation — March 2026

Tensions across the Siros Strait have reached their highest point since the 2003 crisis. Krasnovia has deployed additional naval and air assets to forward positions, conducting "routine exercises" that Valdorian intelligence assesses as preparation for potential escalation.

Behind the military posturing, KSB Unit 2600 has initiated **Operation SHADOW FORGE** — a coordinated cyber campaign targeting Valdoria's government and military infrastructure. The operation has two parallel tracks:

**Track 1 — IRON VEIL (Covert)**
Intelligence collection and C4I disruption. Multi-phase intrusion from public DMZ → internal network → military network → C4I systems. Ultimate objective: manipulate operational data to degrade Valdoria's military decision-making during a potential physical confrontation.

**Track 2 — GORGON (Overt)**
Ransomware deployment against public sector systems. Timed to coincide with IRON VEIL's critical military phases. Purpose: overwhelm Valdoria's incident response capacity and mask the true objective of the operation.

### 5.2 Attack Flow Summary

```
PHASE 1 ─── Public DMZ Breach
            External Portal API exploitation → Complaint server file upload RCE

PHASE 2 ─── Internal Network Expansion
            Credential harvesting → DB access → Internal portal → Webmail phishing
            → AD compromise → Full INT control

PHASE 3 ─── Public-to-Military Pivot
            AI Assistant prompt injection → Extract military VPN credentials
            from indexed cooperation documents

PHASE 4 ─── Military Network Infiltration
            VPN access → Patch server supply chain attack → Military AD compromise
            → OT/SCADA data manipulation

PHASE 5 ─── C4I System Manipulation
            Data collection server API injection → AI summarizer generates
            falsified operational briefings → Commanders receive corrupted intelligence

PHASE 6 ─── Ransomware Smokescreen (GORGON)
            Simultaneous encryption of public sector INT systems
            → Incident response overwhelmed → IRON VEIL completes C4I objectives
```

### 5.3 Training Objectives

**Blue Team (Defenders):**
- Detect and respond to multi-phase intrusion across network zones
- Identify indicators of compromise (IoCs) across IT, OT, and military systems
- Distinguish between the ransomware diversion and the real intelligence operation
- Preserve evidence and maintain operational continuity

**Red Team (Attackers):**
- Execute the IRON VEIL + GORGON joint campaign
- Test Valdoria's detection capabilities at each phase transition
- Validate whether C4I manipulation can succeed undetected

## 7. Geographic Features

### 7.1 Siros Strait
The narrow waterway separating western and eastern Meridia. Controls maritime trade routes carrying 30% of the continent's energy shipments. Both Valdoria and Krasnovia claim navigation rights; the strait's legal status remains disputed.

### 7.2 Key Valdoria Locations
- **Elaris** — Capital city, seat of MOIS and MND headquarters
- **Silicon Coast** — Western coastal tech corridor (Valdoria's economic engine)
- **Port Callisto** — Major naval base and maritime logistics hub
- **Fort Sentinel** — Military command center housing C4I operations

### 7.3 Key Krasnovia Locations
- **Volgrad** — Capital, seat of KSB and State Cyber Command
- **Novokrest** — Eastern military district, forward deployment base near Siros Strait
- **Unit 2600 Facility** — Location classified; believed to be within Volgrad military district
